WHAT IS AN IPO?

An IPO (Initial Public Offering) is the process by which a private company becomes public, offering its shares to the public for the first time in the stock market.

WHAT ARE THE MAJOR DIFFERENCES BETWEEN A TRADITIONAL IPO AND A TOKENIZED IPO?

A traditional IPO involves issuing the company's shares to investors in exchange for capital. A Tokenized IPO, on the other hand, is a form of public offering in which the company issues tokens, which represent shares of the company, using blockchain technology.

HOW MUCH DOES A TOKENIZED IPO COST?

The costs of a Tokenized IPO can vary depending on several factors, such as the size of the company, legal consulting fees, listing fees on tokenization platforms, the jurisdiction of issuance, among others. The t-IPO by eNor Securities aims to bring, at most, a cost of 50,000 USD for companies.

WHICH TYPE OF STARTUP CAN APPLY FOR A TOKENIZED IPO?

There is no defined minimum revenue to conduct a Tokenized IPO, but companies need to meet regulatory and market requirements to attract investors.

HOW LONG DOES IS TAKE TO ACTUALLY LIST?

The t-IPO by eNor Securities is aimed at Tech, AI, and Web3 Startups from anywhere in the world that have already gone through initial funding. Additionally, the Startups must have active communities and engagement in the digital space, among other requirements.

WHAT ARE THE MAJOR STEPS IN A TOKENIZED IPO PROCESS?

The stages of a Tokenized IPO include the selection of financial and legal advisors, the preparation of offering documents, conducting due diligence, obtaining regulatory approvals, distributing tokens to investors, and listing the tokens on eNor Securities and other trading platforms.

WHAT DOES IT MEAN TO BE "REGULATED" IN THE CASE OF AN TOKENIZED IPO?

Being "regulated" in the case of a Tokenized IPO means that the token offering complies with relevant laws and regulations, which may include securities regulations, investor protection, and anti-money laundering measures.
